Goals to Achieve for the leadership

To be source of erudite individuals, imbued with Buddhist values who contribute to the well
Leading the sons of Ananda through a path of moral and intellectual discovery. Instilling Buddhist values and patriotic consciousness while fulfilling National Educational goals.
Contributing patriotic and religious model citizens to the society through personality development and education.

Developing a sense of Sinhala Buddhist identity while building peaceful dialogue among other religions and nationalities.

Providing the students with the necessary facilities to follow the National Educational Curriculum approved by the Ministry of Education under an environment enriched by Buddhist principles and values.

Equipping the students with a awareness of their rights and responsibilities, social norms, legal and moral obligations that are fundamental to the development of a model society.

Laying the foundation for the students to lead exemplary life style encompassed by religious and economic prosperity.

Instilling the sense of gratitude in scholars towards their alma mater and encouraging them to act in wider community in the same sprit they are nurtured.

Molding aspiring personalities through extracurricular activities and contributing health, efficient and well-disciplined citizens to the society.

Nurturing an identity of Anandians equipping the scholars with the skills and knowledge essential in order to achieve local and recognition.

Crest


History of Ananda Crest runs back to the period of the Principal M U Moore (1910 – 1914) who was an Irish by birth. The original crest created was of shape of shield. As figures, there was a Buddhist Flags at either side. The Motto (in Pal) “Appamado Amathapadang” (that means Diligence leads to Perfection) was written under the figures. It was in the mid 1920’s when former Principal Mr. P. De.S Kularathne altered the location of Pali Motto to the top of the Crest written in Sinhalese with the idea that the motto in Pali that conveys a message of Lord Buddha should not be at the bottom. Consequent a redesign form of the Crest came into be with the Stupa radiating rays depicting spreading of light of the doctrine of Lord Buddha and a lion and a lotus under it reflecting the protection of the Noble Doctring of Lord Buddha in its purity. Mr. M E Fernando receives the honour of redesigning the crest and he is a versatile personality who also composed the Anthem of Ananda College around in 1930’s.
